Environmental Impact

Chemical Leaching and Soil Toxicity:

When electronics (e-waste) or industrial scraps are dumped in unlined landfills, toxic heavy metals like lead, mercury, and cadmium seep into the earth.
This "toxic soup" destroys soil fertility and contaminates groundwater, eventually entering the human food chain

Atmospheric Poisoning:

Landfills are not silent; they are massive chemical reactors. As organic matter decomposes anaerobically (without oxygen), it releases methane, a greenhouse gas
over 80 times more potent than carbon dioxide over a 20-year period. Furthermore, the open burning of scraps
a common practice in regions with poor waste infrastructure—releases dioxins and fine particulate matter that cause chronic respiratory diseases globally.

The Plastic and Microplastic Plague:

Papers and plastics that aren't recycled often find their way into waterways.
Today, an estimated 14 million tons of plastic enter our oceans annually. These don't disappear; they break down into microplastics, which have now been found
in everything from deep-sea organisms to human blood.
